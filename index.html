My Story

I started my career as a lead preschool teacher — and what that taught me, more than anything, was that the same information lands completely differently depending on who's receiving it.

From teaching two year olds emergency procedures to teaching sixty year old truck drivers how to use tablets as federal laws changed their entire workflow — I've spent my career understanding how different people learn, process, and experience things.

That question has followed me everywhere. It's why I build systems before I build solutions. It's why I research before I create. It's why I named my app after a Nahuatl word for the soul.

My Biggest Project

Teyolia — that which gives life to people

In Nahuatl, teyolia means "that which gives life to people" — the vital force that lives in the heart and carries identity, memory, and deepest emotions forward, even beyond death.

I named this project after it because that's exactly what books do. They live in your heart. They shape who you are. They hold feelings you can't find anywhere else.

It started with a developer named Rusty. I messaged him on Instagram about his desktop game. He was generous and encouraging. That conversation sparked everything — what if your little character lived their own library life while you worked?

From there Teyolia grew into a book tracking app, a journal, a community. Her name is Mar — ocean in Spanish. She lives in Willowmere with her cat Sombra.
